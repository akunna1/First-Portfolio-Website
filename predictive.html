Predictive Modeling

What is Predictive Modeling? It is a technique used in data science to forecast future events or behaviors by analyzing patterns and relationships within historical data. Through the application of statistical algorithms and machine learning methods, predictive models utilize past information to make informed predictions about future occurrences.

These models can be employed across various domains, including finance, healthcare, marketing, and more, to anticipate trends, mitigate risks, and optimize decision-making processes. By identifying key factors and understanding their impact on outcomes, predictive modeling empowers organizations to make proactive strategies and allocate resources effectively.

PROJECTS

1. Building a machine learning model to detect credit card fraud

Objective: to develop a machine learning model to differentiate between legitimate and fraudulent credit card transactions. The dataset includes transaction details like amount, time, and principal components obtained through PCA (Principal Component Analysis). The model analyzes these features to classify transactions accurately.

Tools Utilized: Python (seaborn, pandas, numpy, matplotlib, sklearn)

In the context of credit card fraud detection, predictive modeling involves examining past transactions to detect patterns indicative of fraud. It finds applications in various domains such as predicting customer churn, forecasting stock prices, and anticipating equipment failures.

The analysis starts with exploring data to understand the distribution of transactions and statistical measures for each category. Data preprocessing includes splitting the dataset into training and testing sets and visualizing transaction amounts and feature correlations.

Logistic regression is chosen as the machine learning algorithm for binary classification. The model is trained and evaluated using metrics like accuracy on both training and testing data. The goal is to deploy a reliable model to identify fraudulent transactions effectively, aiding financial institutions in preventing fraud and protecting cardholders' interest
