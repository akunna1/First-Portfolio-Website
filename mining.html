Data Mining

What is Data Mining? Data mining is like searching for valuable gems in a big, messy pile of rocks (data). It is about finding useful patterns, trends, or insights hiding within large amounts of information. Just as miners sift through rocks to find precious stones, data miners sift through data to discover valuable knowledge that can help make better decisions or understand how things work.

Data Mining Tools encompass various resources, including software like R or Python, databases such as MySQL or PostgreSQL, and machine learning libraries like scikit-learn or TensorFlow. Analysis can vary from employing statistical methods for smaller datasets to utilizing big data technologies capable of handling massive amounts of information.

1. Topic Modeling of US Presidential Speeches

Objective: to analyze presidential speeches, uncovering themes and differences between political parties through text analysis techniques.

Tools Utilized: R (tidyverse, tidytext, dplyr, topicmodels, stringr, and textstem)

• The analysis begins by installing and loading essential R packages for data manipulation, text analysis, and topic modeling. It then reads a CSV file containing presidential speeches data, focusing on speeches by Democrats and Republicans. Two distinct datasets are created and analyzed using tokenization, stopwords removal, and lemmatization.

•Following this preprocessing, the Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) algorithm is applied to each dataset independently. This analysis offers insights into linguistic and thematic differences between the two political parties, shedding light on their ideologies and policy priorities.

•The analysis highlights stark contrasts in speech topics between Republicans and Democrats. Republicans emphasize reducing government spending, international relations, trade, and drug-related issues. Conversely, Democrats concentrate on American politics, evident in words like "republican," "democratic," "election," "vote," and "party." These differences likely arise from specific events and contexts during each administration, reflecting the prevailing political climate and priorities.
